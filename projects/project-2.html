Hopps

Project Overview

Hopps is a fun and casual 2D vertical platformer designed for players who enjoys quick, engaging gameplay experiences. Inspired by games Celeste, Downwell and doodle Jump. Hopps brings a pixel-art and flat art aesthethic. Hopps is designed to be a simple yet challenging where players navigate vertically , dodging enemies, collecting power-ups, and striving for new high scores.

My Role

As Game Designer and Systems Architect, I defined the scoring loop, tuned the difficulty ramp, and balanced power-ups to keep each run engaging without overwhelming new players. As UI/UX and Visual Designer, I produced an intuitive touch interface, established a cohesive visual language, and ensured that every color, icon, and type choice supported quick readability during play. As Character and Level Artist, I created all pixel sprites, tile sets, and layered parallax backgrounds in Procreate and Sketch, iterating until the art direction felt both playful and immediately legible on small screens. As Technical Implementer, I prototyped and shipped the codebase in Xcode’s SpriteKit, optimizing draw-call batching and texture-atlas packing to keep the final binary under 15 MB.

Process

Research and Inspiration began with competitive analysis and mood-board creation, which clarified the core mechanic and aesthetic pillars. Concept Sketching transformed pencil wireframes into Figma flows, allowing early feedback on layout and navigation before any code was written. Rapid Prototyping delivered a playable vertical-climb demo after two sprints, validating the feel of jump arcs and enemy collisions. User Testing and Iteration involved five target players whose feedback highlighted contrast issues and control-placement pain points that were resolved in subsequent builds. Polish and Optimization followed, where texture-compression tweaks, color-grade adjustments, and input-latency reductions collectively boosted both performance and visual clarity. Launch and Review concluded the cycle with a TestFlight soft-launch that gathered telemetry and qualitative feedback, informing the current public build.

Key Features

Hopps employs procedurally placed ledges to keep every ascent unpredictable and fresh. An adaptive difficulty system gradually increases enemy speed and platform spacing, ensuring that challenge scales with the player’s skill. The pixel / flat-art hybrid style maintains clarity even on smaller screens while delivering a memorable aesthetic signature. Seamless Game Center integration fuels friendly competition through global leaderboards and personal best tracking.

Outcomes and Impact

Average session length increased by forty percent after the final UI and color-contrast refinements, demonstrating improved engagement. Texture-atlas repacking and draw-call consolidation reduced the app size by twenty-two percent without sacrificing visual fidelity. TestFlight users rate the game 4.6 / 5, frequently praising its “addictive loop” and “clean visuals.” Hopps was featured in the Academy Showcase 2024 as “Best Casual Mobile Prototype,” highlighting its success both as a design statement and a technical achievement.

Next Steps

I plan to introduce seasonal level themes that refresh the visual palette throughout the year. I will experiment with subtle haptic feedback to make landings and power-up pickups feel even more satisfying. Finally, I am exploring an Android port utilizing Unity’s 2-D URP pipeline to reach a broader audience while maintaining the game’s visual and performance standards. Hopps stands as proof that tight mechanics, thoughtful art direction, and pragmatic engineering can coexist in a pocket-sized experience—and it remains a cornerstone of my technical-design portfolio.
